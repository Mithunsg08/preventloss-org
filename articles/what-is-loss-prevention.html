Introduction

Loss prevention is the process of identifying, reducing and preventing losses that negatively impact an organization's profitability and operational performance. Modern loss prevention extends beyond theft prevention and includes inventory management, procurement controls, internal audits, compliance monitoring and operational excellence.

Key Insight: Most organizations lose more money through process failures, inventory inaccuracies and procurement leakages than through theft alone.

What Is Loss Prevention?

Loss prevention refers to the systematic approach used to protect assets, inventory, cash, information and operational resources. The objective is to identify risks early and implement controls that minimize losses while improving efficiency.

Why Loss Prevention Matters

Organizations operate with thin margins. Even small leakages across receiving, inventory, procurement or finance can accumulate into significant financial losses over time.
